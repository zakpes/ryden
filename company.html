Sato Shihi is the president, chief executive officer and co-founder of Power Japan Plus. Mr. Shihi brings nearly three decades of experience from some of the world’s leading technology companies to Power Japan Plus.

He previously served as manager of financial and new business development at Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Corporation (NTT), the world’s largest telecommunications company, ranked 29th in the Fortune Global 500. He also served two separate stints at multinational imaging and electronics company RICHO, most recently as a senior specialist of new business development.

In this role, Mr. Shihi advanced a business incubator focused on new energy and eco-friendly technologies. He holds a bachelor of economics from Keio University in Japan.
Hanawa Naonobu is chief operating officer and co-founder of Power Japan Plus, overseeing the operations and overall direction of the company's development, expansion and partnership efforts. Mr. Hanawa has nearly 20 years of experience as a pioneer and entrepreneur in the technology industry. Prior to joining Power Japan Plus in 2014, Mr. Hanawa was the CEO and founder of ADM, a software and system development company for SONY PlayStation. His work from 1995-1999 resulted in the game software, MetaPhList.

After that, Mr. Hanawa served as a consultant for Superscape, Plc., where he developed a 3D engine for cellular companies. From 2000-2007, he founded Synapse Communications, where he developed a solution for artificial intelligence and remote medicine using a Dicom format. Mr. Hanawa is a graduate of Chuo University in Tokyo, where he received a bachelor's degree in international economics.
Onaeda Katsuhito is chief technology officer of Power Japan Plus. Dr. Onaeda wields 27 years of Research and development experience in the areas of advanced materials for photo semiconductors, permanent magnets, multi layer ceramic capacitors, nickel-metal hydride batteries and lithium rechargeable batteries. Dr. Onaeda previously served as manager of the advanced materials group at Sumitomo Metal Mining, where he developed the nickel cobalt aluminum cathode for lithium rechargeable batteries used in the Toyota Prius and Tesla Model S. He also contributed to the establishment of the Niihama Research Laboratories, the main research center of Sumitomo Metal Mining. Dr. Onaeda then led battery development at start-ups including Quallion and EnerDel, designing high-power batteries capable of withstanding use in rigorous applications such as satellites and implantable medical applications. He most recently served as a special term appointee at Argonne National Laboratory, where he oversaw the establishment of the laboratory’s cathode manufacturing pilot line. Dr. Onaeda graduated from Kyushu University with a master’s degree in engineering and holds a PH.D in engineering from Tohoku University in Japan.
