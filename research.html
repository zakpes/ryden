Research

Ryden rediscovered

From the 1970s model of an undeveloped and untried dual carbon battery came Ryden. The fledgling development was dropped when research determined the battery would not be able to hold enough energy for commercial use.

For almost forty years the design was locked away. This was until one day, almost ten years ago when Professor Tatsumi Ishihara began revisiting its flaws and applying new chemistries.

+COMBINING FORTY YEARS OF DEVELOPMENT AND LATEST CHEMISTRIES

A vital partnership

The Ryden Dual Carbon battery came to fruition with one major partnership; Kyushu University in Fukuoka, Japan. As one of the top ranked material science and engineering Universities in the world, Kyuchu was a crucial marriage for this development.

The breakthrough

Like a phoenix from the ashes sprang forth the newly improved and highly sustainable Ryden Dual Carbon battery. Modern science meant the battery could finally be adapted as a usable sustainable energy resource.

Once this connection was made Professor Ishihara then joined with Dr. Kaname Takeya and Power Japan Plus to bring the dual carbon battery to life.
